## beat1.mp3
Before the heat — before the dust lifts gold into the morning — there is only the light. It spills across the plains slow as poured honey, waking a land so wide the eye cannot hold it. Grass running to the very edge of the world. Acacias standing like sentinels in the haze. A silence so deep you might believe nothing here breathes at all. But this stillness is not emptiness. It is a kingdom — ancient, sun-drunk, and ruled. And here, unhurried, comes its sovereign. Certain of every step. Walking the borders of a realm that has always, and only, been his.

## beat2.mp3
The lion. You come expecting fury. Instead you find this — a great body poured into the shade, breathing slow, watching the long day pass through half-closed eyes. He will spend most of it so: as much as twenty hours surrendered to stillness, to the patient arithmetic of strength saved for the moment it is needed. And yet — look closer. The heavy gold of that gaze. The dark cathedral of the mane, fanned about his face like a crown he was born already wearing. No other cat on this earth will ever know its weight. Here is power with nothing left to prove. The king of the savanna, at his ease. But no king holds a kingdom alone.

## beat3.mp3
Alone among the great cats, the lion does not walk the world a stranger to its own kind. The lion belongs — to a pride. And the soul of that pride is its lionesses. Sisters and daughters, mothers and aunts, bound each to each by blood and by a loyalty that outlasts all else. They wake together. They hunt together. They raise their young in one warm tangle of fur and watchful eyes. The great male keeps the edges — a wall of muscle and mane thrown up against the dark. But it is the sisterhood at the centre that truly holds. Remove him, and another will take his place. Remove them, and the pride itself unravels into the grass. This is a life of fierce devotion. And of waiting. For the plains are generous to no one.

## beat4.mp3
When hunger finally stirs the pride, it is the lionesses who rise to meet it. They are not made for the long chase, and they know it. So they hunt as a single mind scattered across many bodies — drifting wide through the gold, low to the ground, each reading the others, the wind, the smallest shiver in the grass. Patience drawn thin as wire. And still, more often than not, the plains win. The prey breaks, the moment shatters, and the hunters melt back into the heat with nothing at all. But they never hunt alone — and that is everything. For what no single lion could bring down, the sisterhood can. Together. Only ever together.

## beat5.mp3
And all of it — the hunger, the patience, the long thin waiting — all of it is for this. Lion cubs. Born into darkness, blind and soft and impossibly small, hidden deep in the grass through the first uncertain weeks of their lives. Watch them tumble and pounce and topple, and you might call it play. It is something far older. Every ambush badly sprung, every clumsy chase, is a lesson — the slow rehearsal of the lion each must one day become. Not all of them will. The savanna is tender with nothing, and its young are its most fragile gift. But those who endure will carry it all forward — the blood, the bond, the kingdom itself — into a season not yet dreamed.

## beat6.mp3
As the light begins to fail, the king climbs to his ground, and speaks. A roar that does not so much break the silence as take possession of it — rolling out across the plains, carrying as far as eight kilometres into the dusk. It is a warning, hurled into the gathering dark for any rival who would dare the borders. And it is a summons — a voice reaching out for his own, wherever the night has scattered them. This land, it says — every blade of it, every shadow — is mine.

## beat7.mp3
And so the day folds closed, the way it opened — in light. The pride draws in. The plains breathe out their heat. The sky turns from gold to ember to a deep and final blue, and the great cats become shapes, and then memory, and then nothing the eye can hold. Tomorrow it begins again. The waking. The hunt. The fierce keeping of the young. The endless defence of a kingdom that asks, of any who would rule it, simply everything. And for as long as these old plains run gold to the horizon — they will never want for a lord.
