# Lion Documentary #01 — Narration Script (v2)

**Working title:** *Lion — Lord of the Savanna*
**Runtime target:** ~10 minutes
**Narrator voice:** deep, measured, poetic British male — read like a poet speaking aloud the
scene before his eyes, unhurried and reverent. Lean into the imagery. Long pauses; let the
footage and the natural ambience breathe between lines.

**How this is built:** each beat lists the footage that plays and the voice-over (VO) over it.
We fit the clips to the VO during assembly (trim, slow Ken-Burns moves, crossfades), so exact
clip lengths aren't needed yet — just flag any beat whose footage is too short for its lines.

**Audio bed:** synthetic ambient wind/grasslands soundscape (built like the ocean stream), low
under the voice; the natural roar plays up in Beat 6. No licensed music.

---

### Beat 1 — Cold open: the kingdom and its sovereign  (~70s)
*Visuals:* aerial savanna (#3) → field with trees (#4) → sunrise reflection (#5) → lion walking in (#6).

> *(open on silence and ambience)*
> Before the heat — before the dust lifts gold into the morning — there is only the light.
>
> *(beat)*
> It spills across the plains slow as poured honey, waking a land so wide the eye cannot hold
> it. Grass running to the very edge of the world. Acacias standing like sentinels in the haze.
> A silence so deep you might believe nothing here breathes at all.
>
> *(beat)*
> But this stillness is not emptiness. It is a kingdom — ancient, sun-drunk, and ruled.
>
> *(the lion walks into frame)*
> And here, unhurried, comes its sovereign. Certain of every step. Walking the borders of a
> realm that has always, and only, been his.

### Beat 2 — The lion at rest  (~75s)
*Visuals:* lion resting close-up (#7) → lion at rest (#8).

> The lion.
>
> *(beat)*
> You come expecting fury. Instead you find this — a great body poured into the shade, breathing
> slow, watching the long day pass through half-closed eyes. He will spend most of it so: as much
> as twenty hours surrendered to stillness, to the patient arithmetic of strength saved for the
> moment it is needed.
>
> *(linger on the face and mane)*
> And yet — look closer. The heavy gold of that gaze. The dark cathedral of the mane, fanned
> about his face like a crown he was born already wearing. No other cat on this earth will ever
> know its weight.
>
> *(beat)*
> Here is power with nothing left to prove. The king of the savanna, at his ease. But no king
> holds a kingdom alone.

### Beat 3 — The pride  (~100s)
*Visuals:* lioness in savanna (#11) → lion & lioness in the fields (#9) → the pair (#10).

> Alone among the great cats, the lion does not walk the world a stranger to its own kind. The
> lion belongs — to a pride.
>
> *(over the lionesses)*
> And the soul of that pride is its lionesses. Sisters and daughters, mothers and aunts, bound
> each to each by blood and by a loyalty that outlasts all else. They wake together. They hunt
> together. They raise their young in one warm tangle of fur and watchful eyes.
>
> *(over the pair)*
> The great male keeps the edges — a wall of muscle and mane thrown up against the dark. But it
> is the sisterhood at the centre that truly holds. Remove him, and another will take his place.
> Remove them, and the pride itself unravels into the grass.
>
> *(beat)*
> This is a life of fierce devotion. And of waiting. For the plains are generous to no one.

### Beat 4 — The hunt  (~100s)
*Visuals:* lions roaming (#13) → lion & lioness, a different moment (#12 / reuse #9).

> When hunger finally stirs the pride, it is the lionesses who rise to meet it.
>
> *(slow, tense)*
> They are not made for the long chase, and they know it. So they hunt as a single mind
> scattered across many bodies — drifting wide through the gold, low to the ground, each reading
> the others, the wind, the smallest shiver in the grass. Patience drawn thin as wire.
>
> *(beat)*
> And still, more often than not, the plains win. The prey breaks, the moment shatters, and the
> hunters melt back into the heat with nothing at all.
>
> *(beat)*
> But they never hunt alone — and that is everything. For what no single lion could bring down,
> the sisterhood can. Together. Only ever together.

### Beat 5 — The cubs  (~75s)
*Visuals:* cub on a rock (#14) → cubs (#15).

> And all of it — the hunger, the patience, the long thin waiting — all of it is for this.
>
> *(over the cubs, tender)*
> Lion cubs. Born into darkness, blind and soft and impossibly small, hidden deep in the grass
> through the first uncertain weeks of their lives.
>
> *(beat)*
> Watch them tumble and pounce and topple, and you might call it play. It is something far older.
> Every ambush badly sprung, every clumsy chase, is a lesson — the slow rehearsal of the lion
> each must one day become.
>
> *(beat, gentle and honest)*
> Not all of them will. The savanna is tender with nothing, and its young are its most fragile
> gift. But those who endure will carry it all forward — the blood, the bond, the kingdom itself
> — into a season not yet dreamed.

### Beat 6 — The roar  (~60s)
*Visuals:* lion standing on a rock (#17) → lion / mane portrait (#18) → the roar (#16, natural sound up).

> As the light begins to fail, the king climbs to his ground, and speaks.
>
> *(let the roar play — natural sound up)*
> A roar that does not so much break the silence as take possession of it — rolling out across
> the plains, carrying as far as eight kilometres into the dusk.
>
> *(beat)*
> It is a warning, hurled into the gathering dark for any rival who would dare the borders. And
> it is a summons — a voice reaching out for his own, wherever the night has scattered them.
>
> *(beat, low)*
> This land, it says — every blade of it, every shadow — is mine.

### Beat 7 — Golden-hour close  (~60s)
*Visuals:* lion strolling in golden light (#20) → golden sunset (#19).

> And so the day folds closed, the way it opened — in light.
>
> *(over the strolling lion)*
> The pride draws in. The plains breathe out their heat. The sky turns from gold to ember to a
> deep and final blue, and the great cats become shapes, and then memory, and then nothing the
> eye can hold.
>
> *(beat)*
> Tomorrow it begins again. The waking. The hunt. The fierce keeping of the young. The endless
> defence of a kingdom that asks, of any who would rule it, simply everything.
>
> *(final, over the sunset)*
> And for as long as these old plains run gold to the horizon — they will never want for a lord.
>
> *(fade to black; ambience falls away)*

## Notes
- **Facts used** (all well-established; flag any to verify at approval): lions rest up to ~20
  hours/day; the mane is unique to lions among cats; lions are the only truly social cat, living
  in prides; lionesses are related and do most of the cooperative hunting; most hunts fail; cubs
  are born helpless with high mortality; a lion's roar carries up to ~8 km / 5 miles.
- **Word count** ~780 — lyrical but still leaving room for ambience and long pauses across ~10 min.
